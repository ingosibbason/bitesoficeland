Our suppliers

Urta Islandica

Urta Islandica ltd. is a family business founded in January 2013, but the brand: urta.islandica has been used since October 2010. Thora Thorisdottir Artist is the founder of the company which is co-owned by Sigurdur Magnusson engineer, Lilja Sigrun Jonsdottir Artist, Holmfridur Thorisdottir Philosopher, Sigrun Birta Sigurdardottir peace educator, Gudbjorg Lara Sigurdardottir Designer, Kolbeinn Larus Sigurdsson Engineer and Thangbrandur Humi Sigurdsson a Student. Urta Islandica specialises in products made from Icelandic herbs. Our products are herbal tea, herbal salt, jams and syrup. Urta Islandica has two retail stores and sells their products in Gourmet stores all around Iceland. The products are made in Reykjanesbær, and the company is a certified member of the Reykjanes Geopark. Urta Islandica is sustainable and environmental friendly company. The aim is to limit the use of plastic as much as possible, by packing the gourmet products in glass that can be returned to the company and in Natureflex, home-compostable material. The company is a part of Kolviður - The Iceland Carbon Fund (ICF) offers carbon offsets through tree planting.

Saltverk

The word remote might have been invented to describe the Westfjords of Iceland. After finishing my studies I wanted to create something that honored and built upon traditional Icelandic food methods in a sustainable way. The Danish king established salt production in the 18th century using geothermal energy to produce salt. The production stopped a few decades later and the tradition of saltmaking in Iceland came to an end.

Smáris Volcano Sauce

Anyone can blend a Carolina Reaper together with some chili, vinegar and salt and you have a very spicy mix, but if it's just spicy and at the same time doesn't taste amazing, why would you toss that with your wings? Our spicy philosophy at Smári's Volcano Sauce, is taste first, then bring the heat. We carefully select our raw products to a high standard, we treat them with the respect they deserve, then we grill, infuse, blanche, cook, chill, mix, blend and bottle in our kitchen in Kópavogur, to later ship to heatonist alike all over the world.

Kandís

Kandís is a passion project inspired by Icelandic flora. We handcraft hard candies using only natural ingredients and traditional methods, creating unique flavours with Icelandic herbs and berries.

Omnom

Created by two friends in Reykjavík, Iceland, in 2013, Omnom is led by passionate chef Kjartan Gíslason and entrepreneur Óskar Þórðarson. Omnom produces small-batch, “bean-to-bar” chocolate, using the finest cacao beans sourced worldwide. Together, Gíslason and Þórðarson challenge the understanding of bean-to-bar chocolate production, and the way flavors and textures can creatively expand the business.

Icemart

Icewear has a long tradition of producing wool clothing, starting out as a little wool knitting factory in the early 1970´s. The traditional way of working with yarn gave them great knowledge of Norwegian-style, Scandinavian and in general, Nordic compositions. Nordic designed pullovers and blankets with authentic patterns have a big place in Icelandic culture. Hagkaup

Hagkaup was founded in 1959 and has deep roots in Icelandic society, being an integral part of the country's retail history for about 60 years. Initially, Hagkaup was only a mail-order business, but in 1967, the first physical store was opened at Miklatorg. The first large-scale market of Hagkaup opened in Skeifan in 1970 and is still the most popular store among the seven stores to this day.
